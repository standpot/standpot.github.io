DeSeller8 is a single-player 2D side-scrolling platformer that explores the hidden impact of fast fashion, clothing waste, and consumer choice. Set in a world dominated by Synthetic Fiber Generation (SFG), the game follows the player's journey to challenge a system built on overproduction, careless consumption, and the illusion of "easy" donation.

## Interactive Sustainability

Through pixel-art visuals, platforming mechanics, and story-driven progression, DeSeller8 turns sustainability into an interactive experience. Rather than presenting environmental issues as distant facts, the game invites players to move through them, question them, and make decisions within them.

Each stage is designed to connect action with awareness, showing how everyday fashion choices can affect communities, ecosystems, and the world beyond the closet.

## Development

The project was created with Unity as an HTML5 browser game and combines action, education, and narrative design. Its visual direction uses retro-inspired pixel art to make the subject approachable, while the gameplay keeps the experience active and engaging.

By blending entertainment with social impact, DeSeller8 demonstrates how games can become a medium for reflection, advocacy, and behavioral change.

## Recognition

DeSeller8 was recognized as Game of the Year at the 2024 Games for Change Student Challenge, where it was highlighted for addressing the environmental and social consequences of clothing donation and fast fashion.

The project reflects my interest in designing games that are not only fun to play, but also meaningful to remember.
